# Western Ontario Osteoarthritis of the Shoulder Index (WOOS)

The WOOS is a 19-item patient-reported questionnaire designed to measure the quality of life of patients with osteoarthritis of the shoulder. Each question uses a Visual Analogue Scale (VAS) from 0 to 100.

## Questionnaire

The WOOS consists of 19 questions divided into 4 sections:

### Physical Symptoms (Questions 1–6)

Maximum score: 600. Covers pain, stiffness, and other physical symptoms.

### Sports/Recreation/Work (Questions 7–11)

Maximum score: 500. Covers limitations in sports, recreational activities, and work.

### Lifestyle (Questions 12–16)

Maximum score: 500. Covers impact on daily lifestyle activities.

### Emotions (Questions 17–19)

Maximum score: 300. Covers emotional well-being related to the shoulder condition.

## Scoring

- **Total Score**: Sum of all 19 items. Range: 0–1900.
- **Percentage Score**: Calculated as `(1900 - total) / 19`. Range: 0–100.

## Interpretation

- **0%** = worst possible outcome (maximum symptoms)
- **100%** = best possible outcome (no symptoms)

A higher percentage indicates better shoulder function and quality of life.
