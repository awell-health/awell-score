# Western Ontario Osteoarthritis of the Shoulder Index (WOOS)

## Introduction

The WOOS is a patient-administered, disease-specific questionnaire for measuring the quality of life of patients with osteoarthritis of the shoulder. It was developed by Kirkley, Griffin, and Lo at the Fowler Kennedy Sport Medicine Clinic, University of Western Ontario.

The questionnaire consists of 19 items across 4 domains, each answered on a 100 mm Visual Analog Scale (VAS).

## Domains

| Domain | Items | Max score |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Physical Symptoms | Q1–Q6 | 600 |
| Sports/Recreation/Work | Q7–Q11 | 500 |
| Lifestyle | Q12–Q16 | 500 |
| Emotions | Q17–Q19 | 300 |
| **Total** | **Q1–Q19** | **1900** |

## Scoring

1. Each VAS item is scored from 0 (no symptoms) to 100 (extreme symptoms).
2. Section subtotals are the sum of item scores within each domain.
3. The raw total is the sum of all 19 items (range 0–1900). A score of 1900 signifies extreme decrease in shoulder-related quality of life; a score of 0 indicates no decrease.
4. The percentage of normal is calculated as: **(1900 − raw total) / 19**, yielding a value from 0% (worst) to 100% (asymptomatic).
