# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Doctor/Surgeon

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Doctor/Surgeon scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients about their experience with the surgeon who performed their most recent procedure using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items asking whether the doctor/surgeon:

1. Acted professionally
2. Spoke in a way that was easy to understand
3. Answered all questions
4. Treated the patient with respect
5. Made the patient feel comfortable
6. Involved the patient in decisions about treatment
7. Listened and understood concerns
8. Helped understand what was best for the patient
9. Was available when the patient was worried
10. Spent enough time with the patient

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
