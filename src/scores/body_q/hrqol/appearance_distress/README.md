# BODY-Q - Appearance-Related Psychosocial Distress

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Appearance-Related Psychosocial Distress scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their agreement with statements about appearance-related distress using a 4-point response scale (1 = Strongly Disagree to 4 = Strongly Agree).

## Questions

The scale contains 8 items:

1. I am unhappy about my appearance
2. I feel stressed about my appearance
3. I feel depressed about my appearance
4. I feel anxious when people look at me
5. I am worried about not looking normal
6. I am worried about being ugly
7. I tend to avoid being around people
8. I have little interest in doing things

## Calculation

**Higher scores reflect more appearance-related psychosocial distress** (unlike most other BODY-Q scales). If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (less distress) to 100 (more distress) using the conversion table below.
