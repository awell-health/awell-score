# BODY-Q - Body Image

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Body Image scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their agreement with statements about their body image over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Strongly Disagree to 4 = Strongly Agree).

## Questions

The scale contains 7 items:

1. I have a positive body image
2. My body is not perfect but I like it
3. I am happy with my body
4. I am proud of my body
5. I think my body is attractive
6. I feel good about my body when I am naked
7. I have the body I wanted to have

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
