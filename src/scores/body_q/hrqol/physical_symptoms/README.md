# BODY-Q - Physical Symptoms

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Physical Symptoms scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It is a symptom checklist that asks patients how often they experienced various physical symptoms over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = All of the Time to 4 = Never).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items asking how often the patient experienced:

1. Feeling tired during the day
2. Back pain
3. Joint pain
4. Pain or discomfort in legs
5. Loss of balance
6. Feeling weak
7. Shortness of breath with light exercise
8. Swollen feet
9. Itchy or infected skin
10. Heavy sweating

## Calculation

Unlike most other BODY-Q scales, the Physical Symptoms scale does **not** use a Rasch conversion table. It is scored by summing items to obtain the total raw score. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values before summing.
