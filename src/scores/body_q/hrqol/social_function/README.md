# BODY-Q - Social Function

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Social Function scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their agreement with statements about social functioning over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Strongly Disagree to 4 = Strongly Agree).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items:

1. I feel comfortable in a group of people I know
2. People listen to what I have to say
3. I feel accepted by people
4. I feel included when I am in a group
5. I make a good first impression
6. I take part in life rather than watching from the sidelines
7. It is easy for me to make new friends
8. I feel confident in a group setting (e.g. meetings)
9. I am relaxed around people I do not know well
10. I feel confident walking into a room full of strangers

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
