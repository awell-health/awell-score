# BODY-Q - Sexual Function

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Sexual Function scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their agreement with statements about sexual function using a 4-point response scale (1 = Strongly Disagree to 4 = Strongly Agree).

## Questions

The scale contains 5 items:

1. Sex is fulfilling for me
2. I am comfortable undressing in front of my partner
3. I am satisfied with my sex life
4. I am comfortable keeping the lights on during sex
5. I feel sexually attractive when undressed

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
