# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Nipples

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Nipples scale is part of the BODY-Q Chest Module. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their nipples over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied). If the patient's nipples differ from one side to the other, they are instructed to answer based on the side they are least satisfied with.

## Questions

The scale contains 5 items asking about satisfaction with:

1. Shape of the nipples
2. Size of the nipples
3. How flat the nipples are
4. Nipples being visible through a tight t-shirt
5. Appearance of the nipples without clothing

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
