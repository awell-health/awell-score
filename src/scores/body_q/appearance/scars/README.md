# BODY-Q - Appraisal of Body Contouring Scars

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Appraisal of Body Contouring Scars scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients how bothered they have been by various aspects of their body contouring scars over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Extremely Bothered to 4 = Not at All Bothered).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items asking how bothered the patient was by:

1. Having to dress to hide scars
2. How wide the scars look
3. Location of the scars
4. Length of the scars
5. How noticeable the scars are
6. Color of the scars
7. How thick the scars look (e.g. raised or lumpy)
8. Twisted appearance of the scars (e.g. not in a straight line)
9. People seeing the scars
10. Appearance of the scars when not covered by clothing

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
