# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Chest

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Chest scale is part of the BODY-Q Chest Module. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their chest area over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied). If the patient's chest differs from one side to the other, they are instructed to answer based on the side they are least satisfied with.

## Questions

The scale contains 10 scored items plus 1 unscored scar item:

1. Appearance of chest in a loose t-shirt
2. Appearance of chest when lying on back
3. How flat the chest looks when standing up straight
4. Masculine appearance of the chest
5. Appearance of chest during movement (e.g. running, jumping)
6. Appearance of chest in a tight t-shirt
7. Shape of chest without clothing
8. Appearance of chest when bending forward
9. Appearance of chest from the side (profile) without clothing
10. Appearance of chest in a mirror without clothing

**Additional unscored item (for patients who have had chest surgery):**

11. Appearance of surgical scars (not included in scoring)

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. The scar item (Q11) is **not** included in the scoring. If missing data is less than 50% of the 10 scored items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
