# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Abdomen

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Abdomen scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their abdomen over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied).

## Questions

The scale contains 7 items asking about satisfaction with:

1. How clothes fit the abdomen
2. Size of the abdomen
3. Appearance of the abdomen from the side (profile)
4. Shape of the abdomen
5. Appearance of the abdomen in a swimsuit
6. How toned the abdomen looks
7. Appearance of the abdomen when naked

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
