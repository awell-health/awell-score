# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Upper Arms

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Upper Arms scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their upper arms over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied).

## Questions

The scale contains 7 items asking about satisfaction with:

1. Size of the arms
2. How smooth the arms look
3. Shape of the arms
4. Appearance of the skin on the arms
5. How toned the arms look
6. Appearance of the arms when raised
7. Appearance of the arms when uncovered (e.g. in a sleeveless shirt)

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
