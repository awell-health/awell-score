# BODY-Q - Appraisal of Stretch Marks

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Appraisal of Stretch Marks scale is part of the BODY-Q. It asks patients how bothered they have been by various aspects of their stretch marks over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Extremely Bothered to 4 = Not at All Bothered).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items asking how bothered the patient was by:

1. Not being able to wear certain clothes because of stretch marks
2. Width of stretch marks
3. Having to dress to hide stretch marks
4. Length of stretch marks
5. Location of stretch marks (where they are on the body)
6. Stretch marks making the body look older
7. How visible stretch marks are
8. Amount of stretch marks
9. Other people seeing stretch marks
10. Appearance of stretch marks up close

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
