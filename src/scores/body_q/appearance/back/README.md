# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Back

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Back scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their back over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied).

## Questions

The scale contains 4 items asking about satisfaction with:

1. How smooth the back looks
2. Appearance of the back from different angles
3. How toned the back looks
4. Appearance of the back when naked

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
