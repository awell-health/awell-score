# BODY-Q - Satisfaction with Body

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Satisfaction with Body scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients to rate their satisfaction with various aspects of their body as a whole over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Very Dissatisfied to 4 = Very Satisfied).

## Questions

The scale contains 10 items asking about satisfaction with:

1. Appearance of body when dressed
2. How clothes fit
3. Body weight
4. Shape of body
5. Appearance of body in photos
6. Appearance of body from behind
7. Appearance of body from the side (profile)
8. Appearance of body in summer clothes (e.g. shorts, t-shirts)
9. Appearance of body in a swimsuit
10. Appearance of naked body in a mirror

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
