# BODY-Q - Appraisal of Excess Skin

## Introduction

The BODY-Q is a patient-reported outcome measure (PROM) developed using Rasch Measurement Theory (RMT) to evaluate outcomes in bariatric and body contouring surgery. The Appraisal of Excess Skin scale is one of 30 independently functioning scales within the BODY-Q. It asks patients how bothered they have been by various aspects of their excess skin over the past week using a 4-point response scale (1 = Extremely Bothered to 4 = Not at All Bothered).

## Questions

The scale contains 7 items asking how bothered the patient was by:

1. Excess skin making them look heavier than they are
2. Having to dress to hide excess skin
3. Not being able to wear certain clothes because of excess skin
4. How much excess skin hangs
5. Amount of excess skin
6. People seeing excess skin
7. Appearance of excess skin when naked

## Calculation

Higher scores reflect a better outcome. If missing data is less than 50% of the scale's items, the mean of the completed items is inserted for missing values. The raw summed score is then converted to a Rasch-transformed score from 0 (worst) to 100 (best) using the conversion table below.
